My design process

The user interface has quickly become the place where important decisions are made. The modern designer needs to be aware of business use cases, software engineering constraints and most importantly, what the user needs. Balancing all of these can be tricky at times.

This leads to the logical conclusion that designers must have a strong understanding of what the user wants. The most effective way to accomplish this is to receive a range of inputs. In no particular order:

  1. Learn who in the business has a lot of domain knowledge. This is generally (but not always) a software engineer or someone in a BA type role. I build strong relationships with them in order to have a strong understanding of the historical context of the product.
  2. Depending on the business, I cast my net wider to other stakeholders who are in frequent contact with users. This is generally account managers or customer service.
  3. Speaking to users directly. This is the best way to energise your design efforts.

  • Using Figma/Sketch/etc for visual prototypes, HTML/CSS for interactive prototypes, whiteboards/pen and paper for brainstorming sessions and so on.
